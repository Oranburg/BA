I. The Four Problems of the Firm

Every business association, from a handshake partnership to a multinational corporation, exists to solve coordination challenges. This section breaks down the foundational framework of the casebook. Click on each of the Four Problems below to understand how the law acts as a technology to resolve them.

Attribution

The problem of connecting human action to the artificial firm. A firm cannot act on its own; it requires human agents. Agency Law resolves this by establishing doctrines like Actual Authority and Apparent Authority, determining when a principal is bound by the actions of an agent.
Primary Legal Domain: Restatement (Third) of Agency
